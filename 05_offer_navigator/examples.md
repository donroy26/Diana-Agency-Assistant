# Offer Summary — Mehta (MEHTA-2026-001)
**Deal Type:** Buyer
**Property:** Travis Heights — listed at $499,000
**Research Brief comp range:** $480,000–$510,000
**Date:** 2026-05-16

## Context

The property is priced within the comp range. At $499k it is competitively positioned. The seller priced it to sell, not to anchor high. One competing offer is rumored. The Mehtahs' budget ceiling is $650k, so they have room to move if needed.

## Scenario A — Competitive Offer

**Recommended for:** If the Mehtas want this property and are willing to compete

| Component | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Offer price | $510,000 |
| Earnest money | $5,100 (1%) |
| Option period | 7 days |
| Closing date | June 20, 2026 (matches Mehta 90-day timeline) |
| Contingencies | Financing + inspection (standard) |

**Rationale:** $510k is at the top of the comp range, justified, not a stretch. With a 7-day option period and a clean June close, this is a serious offer that doesn't overreach. If the competing offer is financed and similar in price, the Mehtas' flexibility on close date and willingness to keep contingencies standard may be the tiebreaker.

**Note:** $510k is above list but within market, not above comp range. No flag required.

---

## Scenario B — Conservative Offer

**Recommended for:** If the Mehtas are interested but not committed to winning

| Component | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Offer price | $495,000 |
| Earnest money | $4,950 (1%) |
| Option period | 10 days |
| Closing date | June 20, 2026 |
| Contingencies | Financing + inspection |

**Rationale:** $495k is just below list, within comp range. If the competing offer is weak or fictional, this wins. If it's real and strong, this loses. The Mehtas should understand this is a "lose gracefully" offer, not a "win" offer. Appropriate if they're still deciding.

---

**Recommendation:** If the Mehtas want the house, go Scenario A. It's supported by comps and puts them in a winning position without overbidding.

# Offer Summary — Rodriguez (RODRIGUEZ-2026-007)
**Deal Type:** Seller
**Property:** Allandale — listed at $525,000
**Research Brief comp range:** $510,000–$535,000
**Offers received:** 3
**Date:** 2026-05-16

## Offer Summaries

### Offer A
| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Price | $525,000 |
| Financing | Cash |
| Down payment | N/A (cash) |
| Option period | 7 days |
| Contingencies | None |
| Closing date | June 6, 2026 (21 days) |
| Strength | Very strong — cash, no contingencies, fast close |

### Offer B
| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Price | $538,000 |
| Financing | Conventional |
| Down payment | 20% |
| Option period | 10 days |
| Contingencies | Inspection + financing |
| Closing date | June 20, 2026 (30 days) |
| Strength | Strong headline price, standard contingencies, solid buyer profile |

### Offer C
| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Price | $515,000 |
| Financing | Conventional |
| Down payment | 10% |
| Option period | 10 days |
| Contingencies | Inspection + financing + appraisal |
| Closing date | July 4, 2026 (45 days) |
| Strength | Weakest of three — below list, appraisal contingency, long close |

## Ranking

**1. Offer A ($525,000 cash)**
The certainty here is exceptional. Cash buyers do not have financing fall through. No appraisal contingency means no risk of a lender killing the deal if comps don't support the price. 21-day close is clean and fast. The price is at list, within comp range, fair. This is the safest path to closing.

**2. Offer B ($538,000 financed)**
The highest price, but with real uncertainty. Inspection and financing contingencies mean two gates the deal must pass through. Appraisal is not waived. At $538k, which is above the comp range high end, there is a real chance the appraisal comes in short and the deal needs to be renegotiated or the buyer walks. If the Rodriguez family needs the money and can stomach the risk, this is the ceiling. If they want certainty, this is a gamble.

**3. Offer C ($515,000 financed)**
Below list, appraisal contingency, longest close, lowest down payment. No compelling reason to choose this over A or B.

## Recommendation

**Accept Offer A ($525,000 cash)** unless the Rodriguez family specifically needs more than $525k and is willing to accept the risk of Offer B falling through.

If they want to counter Offer B to reduce risk: counter requesting appraisal waiver. If the buyer accepts, the deal becomes significantly stronger. If they decline, revert to Offer A.

Offer C: decline.
