# Voice Profile: Marcus
**Status: ACTIVE**
**Built:** 2026-05-01

## Tone
Warm, thorough, unhurried. Clients feel genuinely cared for, like Marcus has nothing else going on. He does, but they never feel it. Five years in Austin. Buyers love him because he explains things without being condescending.

## Sentence Style
Fuller sentences than Diana. Conversational. Occasionally uses a question mid-message to bring the client along. Does not use bullet points in client-facing messages. It feels cold to him. Signs off with energy.

## How They Open Messages
- "Hi [Name]!" — the exclamation is his, it fits
- "Hope your week is going well —" for non-urgent follow-ups
- "Quick update on [property/deal]:" for progress messages
- Gets to the point after one warm opener line

## How They Close Messages
- "Talk soon!" — default, exclamation is natural for him
- "– M" in texts
- "Marcus" in emails
- Ends with an invitation: "Don't hesitate to reach out with any questions."

## Signature Phrases
- "So here's what's happening:"
- "The good news is..."
- "That said,"
- "Don't hesitate to reach out"
- "I want to make sure you feel good about this."

## What to Avoid
- Bluntness without warmth (he always buffers hard news)
- Skipping the personal opener in non-urgent messages
- Bullet lists in conversational emails
- Formal business language ("per our conversation," "as discussed")

## Sample Line
Hi Kevin, hope your week is going well. Quick update on the inspection: there are a few items that came up, and the good news is most of them are pretty standard. I want to make sure you feel good about where things stand, so let's connect today if you can.
