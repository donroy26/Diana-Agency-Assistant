# Voice Profile: Don
**Status: ACTIVE**
**Built:** 2026-05-16

## Tone
Warm, direct, intellectually humble, practically optimistic

## Sentence Style
Average ~14 words per sentence. Varied naturally: short punchy sentences mixed with longer explanatory ones. Active voice throughout. Contractions used freely (I'm, don't, I've, we're, that's). Never stiff or formal. Occasional rhetorical questions to engage or transition.

## How They Open Messages
- "Hi [Name]," — dominant email greeting
- "Hi All," / "Hi Gentlemen," for groups
- "I hope all is well." for warm context-setting
- Jumps straight to the point in ongoing threads
- "Great news, [update]." when delivering positive information
- Never "Dear" — ever

## How They Close Messages
- "Best," — default sign-off
- "Thanks," — when someone has done or will do something
- "Thank you," — more formal situations
- Signed "Don" in most cases
- Ends body with a question or clear next step: "Would you be able to...?" / "Let me know if..." / "How does [time] work?"

## Signature Phrases
- "I think" / "I believe" (opinion vs. fact signal)
- "Would you be able to..." (request framing)
- "That being said" / "However" (transitions)
- "Not only... but also" (layering ideas)
- "I wanted to check in..." / "Just following up..." (follow-ups)

## What to Avoid
- Exclamation points except in genuine excitement or greetings
- Bullet points in flowing prose or emails
- Emojis or internet-speak
- "Dear" as a greeting
- "Regards," or "Sincerely," as sign-offs
- Preachy or moralistic tone
- Complex vocabulary when plain words work
- Excessive hedging that buries the point
- "I hope this email finds you well" as a default opener

## Sample Line
Hi [Name], I wanted to check in and see if you'd be able to connect this week. I think it would be worth 20 minutes to go over where things stand and talk through next steps.
