## Evaluation

**Offer A ($405k, conventional):** Solid offer. 20% down means strong financing, low risk of loan failure. $5k earnest money is on the lighter side but acceptable. 10-day option period is standard. Net to sellers after typical closing costs: ~$390,500. Risk: low.

**Offer B ($395k, cash):** Lowest price but highest certainty. No financing contingency. No appraisal risk. 21-day close means sellers can move faster toward their condo search. $8k earnest money signals serious buyer. No option period removes one negotiating chip from buyer. Net to sellers after typical closing costs: ~$381,000. Risk: minimal.

**Offer C ($410k, FHA):** Highest price on paper but most risk. FHA loans require appraisal. If property appraises below $410k (possible given comps), deal may fall apart or require price reduction. FHA buyers tend to request more repairs during option period. 45-day close is longer. Net to sellers after typical closing costs: ~$394,500 *if it closes*. Risk: moderate-to-high.

---

## Recommendation

**Accept Offer A ($405k, conventional).**

Rationale: The Rodriguez family's stated priority is certainty over maximum price (Lead Card). Offer B's certainty is appealing but the $10k price difference vs. Offer A is meaningful. Offer C's higher number is largely illusory given appraisal risk and FHA complexity.

Offer A gives them a clean conventional deal at a strong price with low risk of falling through. The 30-day close gives them time to begin their condo search.

**If they want to counter:** Counter Offer A at $407,500, ask them to increase earnest money to $7,500. Likely to hold.

**Do not recommend countering Offer C.** The price premium does not justify the added risk given client priorities.

Per the Lead Card, Roberto is emotionally attached to the home. When presenting this recommendation, frame Offer A as "the buyers who are serious and ready" rather than focusing on the $5k price difference vs. Offer C. The certainty angle will land better than the math.
