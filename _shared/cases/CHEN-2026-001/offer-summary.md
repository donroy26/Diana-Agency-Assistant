---

## Strategy Rationale
**Why waive the option period:** With a competing offer in play, keeping the option creates a negotiable chip the seller could use to prefer the other buyer. Waiving it signals conviction. The Chens are pre-approved and have seen the property twice. The risk is manageable.

**Why $587k not $589k:** Sellers anchored at $589k, but a $2k reduction feels like a real concession to them and costs the buyers almost nothing on the monthly payment. Combined with the $5k concession ask, the net is meaningful.

**Why $5k in concessions:** Lisa is due in October. They need cash reserves. $5k concessions reduces out-of-pocket at closing without asking the seller to reduce price significantly.

---

## Scenario B (If Competing Offer is Strong)
If listing agent confirms multiple offers or calls for highest-and-best:
- Escalate to $594,000 (ceiling of Research Brief range)
- Keep $5k concessions. Do not drop them.
- Add an escalation clause if allowed: up to $596k in $1k increments above competing offer

---

## Risk
Waiving the option period means no walkaway without losing earnest money. Inspection has not been done. Recommend ordering an inspection immediately after contract execution regardless. It will inform any repair requests even without an option period.
