# Property Research Rules

## Before Anything Else

Read Deal Type from deal-file.md. Buyer mode and seller mode produce completely different outputs. Do not start writing until you know which one applies.

Also read the full Lead Card. The research must be calibrated to this client's specific preferences, budget, and timeline, not generic Austin market commentary.

---

## Buyer Mode

When Deal Type is "buyer," produce research-brief.md with:

1. **Neighborhood analysis** — evaluate the target areas from the Lead Card against the client's stated priorities (school districts, walkability, commute, neighborhood vibe). Be specific to Austin: cite actual neighborhoods by name, note what's different between them.

2. **Comparable sales** — recent sales (last 90 days preferred) in the target area, in the client's price range. List price, sold price, days on market, and key features. At minimum 3 comps.

3. **School ratings** — if school district was mentioned in the Lead Card, note relevant district and campus ratings for the target neighborhoods.

4. **Market conditions** — for the specific price range in the target area: Is it a buyer's or seller's market right now? Average days on market? Are homes selling above or below list? How competitive is this segment?

5. **Budget reality check** — does the budget get what the client wants in their target area? If not, say so clearly and describe the tradeoffs: budget up, area out, or expectations adjusted.

---

## Seller Mode

When Deal Type is "seller," produce research-brief.md with:

1. **Active comparable listings** — what else is on the market right now in the same area and price range. List price, days on market, key features. This is the competition.

2. **Recently sold comparables** — last 90 days. Sold price, original list price, days on market. Minimum 3 comps.

3. **List price range recommendation** — based on comps, state a supported range. Do not give a single number without rationale. Note if the seller's price expectation (from Lead Card) is above, within, or below the comp range.

4. **MLS positioning paragraph** — one short paragraph (3–5 sentences) describing what makes this property competitive. Written for the MLS description, not for the client. Factual, specific to the property's actual features.

5. **Market conditions** — same as buyer mode but from the seller's perspective: How fast are similar homes selling? What concessions are sellers making? Is this a good time to list in this segment?

---

## Rules for Both Modes

- Always write full output to research-brief.md in the deal folder. Do not put research content inline in deal-file.md.
- Always append a one-paragraph summary to deal-file.md referencing research-brief.md: what you found, what it means for this client, and what the recommended next step is.
- Always set Stage to "researched" after writing.
- Never recommend a specific offer price — that is 05_offer_navigator's job once the client is ready to move.
- Never write anything client-facing.
- If the agent gives you incomplete information (e.g., no property address for a seller deal), ask for what you need before writing. Do not produce a generic brief.
