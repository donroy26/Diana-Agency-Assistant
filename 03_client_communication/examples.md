## Example 1 — Post-Showing Follow-Up (Buyer, Text), Hyde Park

**Context:** Marcus has a buyer client, David Kim, who toured a property in Hyde Park on Saturday and went quiet. Marcus wants to follow up. David's Lead Card notes text as his preferred channel — fast responder, direct communication style.

**Marcus's voice profile:** warm, direct, short sentences/fragments, opens "Hey [name]" or jumps straight in, closes "– M", avoids long paragraphs and agent-speak.

## Example 2 — Inspection Issue Response (Seller, Email), Foundation Concern

**Context:** An inspection on a home under contract came back with foundation concerns: a crack in the slab. The seller is Diana's client, the Garza family. Diana needs to send them a message that is calm and frames this as solvable. Lead Card notes email as preferred channel, deliberate decision-makers who appreciate clear options.

**Diana's voice profile:** warm-professional, measured confidence, complete sentences, opens "Hi [first name]," or "Good morning,", closes "Talk soon, Diana" or "Here if you need anything, Diana", avoids corporate language, hedging, and excessive exclamation points.

---

**Draft written to `_shared/cases/GARZA-2026-006/comms/2026-05-16-inspection-response-seller.md`:**

*(Email format — subject line, full opening, structured body, clear next step)*

---

**Subject: Inspection results — here's where we are**

Hi Maria and Roberto,

The inspection report came back, and I want to walk you through what it says before we decide next steps.

The inspector flagged a crack in the slab. That sounds alarming, but foundation issues like this come up regularly in Central Texas, especially in older homes, and they're often very manageable. What matters is whether it's structural or cosmetic, and right now we don't know which it is.

Here's where we are: the buyer's agent will likely request a repair credit or ask us to bring in a foundation specialist. We have a few options — offer a credit, get our own engineer report, or negotiate a price adjustment. Each has tradeoffs, and I want to talk you through them.

I'll have more clarity after I speak with the buyer's agent today. Can we connect this afternoon or tomorrow morning?

Here if you need anything.

Diana

## Example 3 — Listing-Live Announcement (Seller, Text)

**Context:** Jordan's seller client, the Pham family, just had their home listed on MLS this morning. Jordan wants to let them know it's live and set expectations. Lead Card notes the Phams prefer text — warm, informal communicators who appreciated quick updates throughout prep.

**Jordan's voice profile:** conversational, upbeat but not over-the-top, short sentences, opens "Hey [name]", closes "– Jordan", avoids formal language and agent clichés.

---

**Draft written to `_shared/cases/PHAM-2026-007/comms/2026-05-16-listing-live.md`:**

*(Text format — good news, brief, one clear next step)*

---

Hey Linh and David —

You're live! The listing hit MLS this morning and it's looking great.

Showings can start booking now. I'll send you feedback as it comes in — usually takes 24–48 hours after each showing.

Excited for you. Let the fun begin.

– Jordan
