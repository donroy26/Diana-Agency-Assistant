## Example 1 — Post-Showing Follow-Up (Buyer), Hyde Park

**Context:** Marcus has a buyer client, David Kim, who toured a property in Hyde Park on Saturday and went quiet. Marcus wants to send a follow-up text, not pushy, just checking in.

**Marcus's voice profile (from `_shared/voice/Marcus.md`):**
- Tone: warm, direct
- Sentence style: short sentences, occasional fragments
- Opens: "Hey [first name]" or jumps straight to the point
- Closes: "– M"
- Signature phrases: "just checking in," "let me know," "no rush"
- What to avoid: long paragraphs, overly formal language, generic agent-speak

---

**Draft written to `_shared/cases/KIMD-2026-005/comms/2026-05-16-post-showing-followup.md`:**

---

Hey David —

Wanted to check in after Saturday. What'd you think of the Hyde Park place?

No pressure. Just want to know if it's still in the running or if we're looking in a different direction.

Let me know when you get a chance.

– M

## Example 2 — Inspection Issue Response (Seller), Foundation Concern

**Context:** An inspection on a home under contract came back with foundation concerns: a crack in the slab. The seller is Diana's client, the Garza family. Diana needs to send them a message that is calm and frames this as a solvable situation, not a disaster.

**Diana's voice profile (from `_shared/voice/Diana.md`):**
- Tone: warm-professional, measured confidence
- Sentence style: complete sentences, moderate length, clear structure
- Opens: "Hi [first name]," or "Good morning,"
- Closes: "Talk soon, Diana" or "Here if you need anything, Diana"
- Signature phrases: "here's where we are," "a few options," "we've handled this before"
- What to avoid: corporate-sounding language, hedging, excessive exclamation points

---

**Draft written to `_shared/cases/GARZA-2026-006/comms/2026-05-16-inspection-response-seller.md`:**

---

Hi Maria and Roberto,

The inspection report came back, and I want to walk you through what it says before we decide next steps.

The inspector flagged a crack in the slab. That sounds alarming, but foundation issues like this come up regularly in Central Texas, especially in older homes, and they're often very manageable. What matters is whether it's structural or cosmetic, and right now we don't know which it is.

Here's where we are: the buyer's agent will likely request a repair credit or ask us to bring in a foundation specialist. We have a few options: we can offer a credit, get our own engineer report, or negotiate a price adjustment. Each has tradeoffs.

I'll have more clarity after I talk with the buyer's agent today. I wanted you to hear this from me first, not through the grapevine.

Here if you need anything.

Diana
